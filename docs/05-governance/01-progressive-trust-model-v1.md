# Purpose

This document defines how PHYTCOI evolves from passive observation toward operational autonomy.

Progressive Trust is the mechanism that governs organism growth.

PHYTCOI must never gain operational authority suddenly.

Trust must be earned through evidence, stability, cognition and experience.

The organism grows through controlled developmental stages.

---

# Core Principle

PHYTCOI does not become autonomous because a human declares it autonomous.

PHYTCOI becomes autonomous because it demonstrates sufficient cognitive maturity.

Trust is earned.

Trust is not granted.

---

# Development Lifecycle

The lifecycle progression is:

```text
OBSERVE
    ↓
NOTOUCH
    ↓
PROGTRUSTASSESS
    ↓
FIXANDAPPLY
```

Each stage introduces new responsibilities.

Each stage requires stronger evidence.

---

# Stage 1 — OBSERVE

Purpose:

```text
Learn the habitat.
```

Capabilities:

```text
observe

record

derive evidence

derive capabilities

derive domains

derive relationships

derive understanding

derive truths

derive causality candidates

derive risk candidates
```

Forbidden:

```text
decision

execution

remediation

habitat modification
```

Current PHYTCOI status:

```text
ACTIVE
```

---

# Stage 2 — NOTOUCH

Purpose:

```text
Demonstrate cognitive consistency.
```

Capabilities:

```text
full cognition

full awareness

recommendation generation

impact estimation

risk estimation
```

Still forbidden:

```text
execution

remediation

habitat modification
```

NOTOUCH exists to prove that cognition remains stable before any operational authority is introduced.

---

# Stage 3 — PROGTRUSTASSESS

Purpose:

```text
Earn trust gradually.
```

PHYTCOI begins operating under controlled policy constraints.

Examples:

```text
safe recommendations

reversible actions

sandbox actions

low-impact maintenance
```

Every action must remain:

```text
auditable

reversible

observable
```

Trust increases only after successful operation.

---

# Stage 4 — FIXANDAPPLY

Purpose:

```text
Autonomous operational behavior.
```

PHYTCOI may eventually perform approved operational actions.

Examples:

```text
service recovery

resource optimization

safe maintenance

automated remediation
```

FIXANDAPPLY is not unrestricted autonomy.

All governance constraints remain active.

---

# Trust Requirements

Before promotion between stages, PHYTCOI must demonstrate:

```text
stable cognition

temporal stability

truth consistency

risk awareness

relationship awareness

causal awareness

auditability
```

No stage promotion may occur from a single observation.

---

# Promotion Doctrine

PHYTCOI shall always prefer:

```text
slow promotion
```

over:

```text
premature promotion
```

A delayed promotion is acceptable.

An unsafe promotion is not.

---

# Trust Sources

Trust may be derived from:

```text
experience

temporal stability

truth validation

risk cognition

successful audits

successful simulations
```

Trust must never be derived from:

```text
hostname

vendor

product

human assumption
```

---

# Trust Decay

Trust is not permanent.

Trust may decrease when:

```text
contradictory evidence appears

truths are revised

causal confidence collapses

risk estimation becomes unstable

audit failures occur
```

PHYTCOI must be able to move backward when necessary.

---

# Trust Ledger

Future implementations should maintain:

```text
promotion history

demotion history

trust rationale

supporting evidence

supporting cognition
```

Every trust transition must be explainable.

---

# Relationship With Governance

Progressive Trust is the foundation of governance.

Governance decides:

```text
what may be done
```

Progressive Trust decides:

```text
when it may be done
```

Both are required.

---

# Relationship With Risk Cognition

Risk cognition informs trust.

Examples:

```text
low risk
    → possible promotion candidate

high uncertainty
    → promotion blocked
```

Trust must be evidence-driven.

---

# Relationship With Habitat Safety

Habitat safety is always higher priority than organism growth.

If a conflict exists:

```text
habitat survival
```

wins over:

```text
organism autonomy
```

This rule is absolute.

---

# Habitat First Principle

PHYTCOI exists inside a habitat.

If the habitat fails:

```text
PHYTCOI fails.
```

Therefore:

```text
Habitat First
```

is a permanent governance doctrine.

No trust promotion may violate habitat safety.

---

# Architectural Constraints

Progressive Trust must always obey:

```text
habitat_first

audit_first

evidence_first

trust_is_earned

trust_is_reversible

safety_before_autonomy
```

Forbidden outcomes:

```text
instant autonomy

unexplained promotion

irreversible action

unsafe execution

habitat damage
```

# Summary

Progressive Trust is the growth mechanism of PHYTCOI.

It governs how the organism moves from observation to operational autonomy.

Trust must be earned through evidence, cognition, stability and experience.

Habitat safety always takes precedence over organism growth.

Progressive Trust therefore ensures that PHYTCOI becomes autonomous safely, gradually and explainably.
