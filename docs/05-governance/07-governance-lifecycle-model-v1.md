# Purpose

This document defines the complete governance lifecycle of PHYTCOI.

The lifecycle describes how an organism evolves from pure observation to controlled operational autonomy.

The lifecycle is not a technical state.

The lifecycle is a governance state.

---

# Core Principle

Growth must be gradual.

Authority must be earned.

Trust must be earned.

Execution must be constrained.

Habitat safety must always dominate organism autonomy.

---

# Lifecycle States

PHYTCOI recognizes four governance stages:

OBSERVE

NOTOUCH

PROGTRUSTASSESS

FIXANDAPPLY

---

# OBSERVE

Purpose:

Learn reality.

Allowed:

observe

record

analyze

understand

derive truth

derive causality

derive risk

Forbidden:

recommend

approve

execute

modify habitat

Characteristics:

maximum safety

minimum authority

cognitive growth only

---

# NOTOUCH

Purpose:

Demonstrate cognitive stability.

Allowed:

all OBSERVE capabilities

recommendations

planning candidates

impact estimation

risk estimation

Forbidden:

approve

execute

modify habitat

Characteristics:

cognition proven

operations forbidden

recommendation-only stage

---

# PROGTRUSTASSESS

Purpose:

Evaluate operational trustworthiness.

Allowed:

all NOTOUCH capabilities

limited approved execution

sandbox execution

reversible actions

verification activities

Characteristics:

trust accumulation

policy-controlled execution

strict auditability

rollback required

---

# FIXANDAPPLY

Purpose:

Controlled operational autonomy.

Allowed:

approved execution

policy-controlled remediation

continuous verification

operational maintenance

Characteristics:

full governance stack active

authority constrained

policy constrained

approval constrained

habitat constrained

---

# Promotion Requirements

Promotion requires:

stable cognition

stable truth inventory

stable causal inventory

stable risk inventory

policy compliance

auditability

temporal stability

Promotion is never automatic.

---

# Demotion Requirements

Demotion may occur when:

trust decreases

audit failures occur

truth revision invalidates assumptions

risk becomes unstable

governance violations occur

Demotion is a safety mechanism.

---

# Lifecycle Ledger

Future lifecycle records should contain:

current stage

previous stage

promotion reason

demotion reason

supporting evidence

supporting truths

timestamp

---

# Habitat First Rule

No lifecycle promotion may increase habitat risk beyond acceptable limits.

Habitat survival always takes priority over lifecycle advancement.

---

# Governance Relationship

Lifecycle depends on:

authority

policy

approval

risk

trust

Lifecycle never replaces governance.

Lifecycle coordinates governance.

---

# Architectural Constraints

Lifecycle governance must obey:

habitat_first

audit_first

evidence_first

truth_first

trust_is_earned

trust_is_reversible

Forbidden outcomes:
