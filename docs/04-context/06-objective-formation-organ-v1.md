# Objective Formation Organ V1

## Status

Proposed

---

# Purpose

Objective Formation is the first intentional cognitive organ of PHYTCOI.

Its role is not to decide.

Its role is not to execute.

Its role is not to govern.

Its role is to transform assimilated context into objective candidates.

# Inputs

Objective Formation consumes:

* Context records
* Domain awareness
* Domain confidence
* Promotion readiness
* Validation candidate ledger
* World Model awareness

---

# Outputs

Objective Formation produces:

* objective records
* objective awareness
* objective state

---

# Core Principle

Objective Formation answers:

"What should be understood next?"

It does not answer:

"What should be done?"

---

# Objective Candidate

An objective candidate is a cognitive direction.

Example:

* improve understanding of identity_and_authentication
* increase evidence for remote_access
* validate web_services domain

These are learning directions.

Not actions.

---

# Forbidden Outcomes

Objective Formation may never generate:

* decisions
* execution plans
* governance instructions
* approval requests
* habitat modifications

---

# Relationship With Context

Context identifies significance.

Objective Formation identifies direction.

Relationship:

Context
↓
Objective Formation

---

# Relationship With World Model

World Model describes reality.

Objective Formation describes cognitive growth opportunities.

Relationship:

World Model
↓
Objective Formation

---

# Objective Lifecycle

candidate
↓
supported_candidate
↓
stable_candidate

No further transition exists.

Objectives never become executable.

---

# Confidence

Objective confidence derives from:

* evidence quality
* context confidence
* world model confidence
* temporal stability

Confidence is not authority.

---

# Observe Boundary

The organ remains fully inside OBSERVE lifecycle.

It introduces no authority.

---

# Audit Requirements

The organ is healthy when:

* objective state exists
* objective awareness exists
* objective records exist
* objective candidates derive from context
* no executable output exists

---

# Success Condition

PHYTCOI can identify what should be learned next without creating decisions.

---

# Failure Condition

Any objective that becomes a task, action, approval, execution, governance instruction or habitat modification is an architectural violation.
