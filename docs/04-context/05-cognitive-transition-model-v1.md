# Cognitive Transition Model V1

## Status

Proposed

---

# Purpose

The Cognitive Transition Model defines the evolutionary transition between:

```text
COGNITIVE_ORGANISM
```

and

```text
INTENT_CAPABLE_ORGANISM
```

This transition represents the most important cognitive milestone since the creation of the World Model.

Before this transition PHYTCOI can understand reality.

After this transition PHYTCOI begins to understand direction.

---

# Historical Context

The first cognitive era produced:

```text
Observer
Discovery
Memory
Experience
Hypothesis
Knowledge
Relationship
Truth
Understanding
World Model
Self
Observatory
```

The result was:

```text
Cognitive Organism
```

A cognition capable of understanding itself and its habitat.

However the organism still lacks intentional structure.

---

# The Missing Layer

Prior cognition can answer:

```text
What exists?

What happened?

What relationships exist?

What is true?

What do I understand?
```

But it cannot answer:

```text
What matters?

What should be learned next?

What future cognitive direction is desirable?
```

The Cognitive Transition introduces those capabilities.

---

Observer

---

# Context Layer

Context introduces:

```text
relevance
importance
opportunity
limitation
```

Context does not create objectives.

Context identifies significance.

---

# Objective Formation Layer

Objective Formation introduces:

```text
future cognitive direction
```

The organ transforms:

```text
context
```

into:

```text
objective candidates
```

Objectives remain non-executable.

---

# Planner Seed Layer

Planner Seed introduces:

```text
hypothetical future pathways
```

The organ transforms:

```text
objective candidates
```

into:

```text
candidate plans
```

These plans remain entirely observational.

---

# New Cognitive Capability

After transition PHYTCOI becomes capable of:

```text
understanding what should be learned next
```

This is the first form of intentional cognition.

---

# What Does Not Exist Yet

The transition explicitly excludes:

```text
decision making

governance

approval

execution

habitat modification
```

These remain future phases.

---

# Relationship With Governance

Governance does not yet exist.

The chain stops at:

```text
Planner Seed
```

No authority is created.

No actions are generated.

No approval requests are possible.

---

# Relationship With Executor

Executor does not yet exist.

No candidate plan may become executable.

Relationship:

```text
Planner Seed
    X
Executor
```

No connection exists.

---

# Lifecycle

Current lifecycle:

```text
OBSERVE
```

The organism remains inside OBSERVE.

The transition does not change lifecycle.

The transition only expands cognition.

---

# Cognitive Boundaries

The organism remains forbidden from:

```text
decision_engine

governance

approval

execution

habitat_modification
```

The transition does not grant authority.

---

# Success Criteria

The Cognitive Transition succeeds when PHYTCOI can:

* identify relevance
* identify cognitive gaps
* generate objective candidates
* generate candidate plans
* explain candidate plans
* maintain observe-only boundaries

without creating decisions or actions.

---

# Failure Conditions

The transition is considered invalid if:

```text
objectives become executable

plans become executable

authority is introduced

governance appears

execution appears
```

Any of these conditions violates the transition architecture.

---

# Audit Requirements

A successful transition requires:

```text
Context healthy

Objective Formation healthy

Planner Seed healthy

World Model healthy

Self Model healthy
```

The Cognitive Audit must verify these conditions.

---

# Maturity Impact

Before transition:

```text
COGNITIVE_ORGANISM
```

After transition:

```text
INTENT_CAPABLE_ORGANISM
```

This is the first stage where the organism possesses direction.

---

# Architectural Principles

```text
context_before_objectives

objectives_before_planning

planning_before_governance

governance_before_execution

observe_only

authority_last

audit_first

world_model_driven

evidence_driven
```

---

# Expected Outcome

After the Cognitive Transition, PHYTCOI becomes capable of understanding not only reality, but also potential future cognitive growth.

The organism remains completely non-executive.

However, it now possesses the foundations required for the future emergence of Planner, Governance and Executor systems.

This marks the beginning of intentional cognition.
