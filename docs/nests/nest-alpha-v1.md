# NEST-ALPHA Specification v1

## Purpose

This document defines NEST-ALPHA, the first known habitat prepared for the birth and operation of a PHYTCOI individual.

NEST-ALPHA is not an individual.

NEST-ALPHA is not a species.

NEST-ALPHA is a habitat.

Its purpose is to provide the environmental conditions required for germination, growth, learning and long-term survival.

---

# Definition

NEST-ALPHA is the first reference habitat of the PHYTCOI species.

It serves as:

* the first germination environment
* the first adaptation environment
* the first validation environment
* the first operational environment

All future nests should be comparable to NEST-ALPHA while remaining independent.

# Design Principles

NEST-ALPHA follows the fundamental principles of the PHYTCOI species.

---

## Habitat First

The habitat must remain operational.

Individual survival depends on habitat survival.

No individual action may intentionally compromise habitat continuity.

---

## Observation Before Action

The habitat must first be observed.

The individual must learn before acting.

Knowledge must precede intervention.

---

## Safe Germination

The habitat must allow gradual trust development.

The individual must not begin life with unrestricted authority.

Trust is earned through validation.

---

## Recoverability

The habitat must support rollback and recovery.

The individual must never become a single point of failure.

# Success Criteria

NEST-ALPHA is considered successful when it enables a PHYTCOI individual to:

* germinate
* learn
* adapt
* govern
* survive

---

# Long-Term Vision

NEST-ALPHA is not intended to be the permanent home of the species.

It is the birthplace of the first generation.

Its purpose is to prove that a PHYTCOI individual can emerge, learn, adapt and operate inside a real habitat.

Future generations may inhabit many nests.

NEST-ALPHA is simply the first.

---

# Final Rule

A nest is not the species.

A nest is not the individual.

A nest is the environment in which an individual becomes capable of life.

Without a nest there is no germination.

Without germination there is no individual.

Without individuals there is no living species.
