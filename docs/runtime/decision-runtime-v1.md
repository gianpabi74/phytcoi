# PHYTCOI Decision Runtime v1

## Purpose

This document defines how decisions are produced within a PHYTCOI individual.

The Decision Runtime is responsible for transforming truth, understanding and wisdom into actionable choices.

DNA provides facts.

The World Model provides understanding.

Experience provides wisdom.

The Decision Runtime selects a course of action.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Decision is not execution.

Decision is not governance.

Decision is not knowledge.

Decision is choice.

The Decision Runtime exists to evaluate possible actions and select the most appropriate one according to species law, habitat safety and available understanding.

---

# Core Question

The Decision Runtime answers:

Given what is known, what should be done?

---

# Runtime Ownership

Biological Owner:

Decision Cortex

Runtime Owner:

Decision Service

Primary Consumers:

* Governance Runtime
* Execution Runtime
* Cambium
* Risk Systems

Decision produces intent.

It does not perform actions.

---

# Decision Objectives

The Decision Runtime must provide:

* option generation
* option evaluation
* option comparison
* intent selection
* decision traceability
* decision explainability

The purpose of Decision is to choose.

---

# Inputs

The Decision Runtime consumes:

* DNA
* World Model
* Experience
* Risk
* Governance Constraints
* Lifecycle State

Decision must remain grounded in reality.

---

# Outputs

The Decision Runtime produces:

* recommendations
* candidate actions
* candidate adaptations
* execution intents
* governance requests

Decision produces possibilities.

Not authority.

---

# Decision Principle

Every decision begins with alternatives.

No decision may assume only one possible action.

The minimum process is:

Situation

↓

Possible Actions

↓

Evaluation

↓

Selection

↓

Intent

↓

Governance Review

---

# Decision Architecture

The Decision Runtime consists of four layers.

---

# Layer 1 — Situation Assessment

## Purpose

Understand the current situation.

Questions:

What exists?

What is happening?

What is changing?

What risks exist?

The Situation Assessment Layer consumes DNA, World Model and Experience.

---

# Situation Registry

Contains:

* situation identifier
* involved objects
* dependencies
* risk references
* confidence

---

# Layer 2 — Option Generation

## Purpose

Generate candidate actions.

Examples:

Do Nothing

Observe Further

Recommend Action

Execute Recovery

Generate Adaptation

Escalate To Governance

A decision without alternatives is incomplete.

---

# Option Registry

Contains:

* option identifier
* option description
* expected outcome
* confidence
* supporting evidence

---

# Layer 3 — Evaluation Layer

## Purpose

Compare options.

Evaluation dimensions include:

* habitat impact
* risk impact
* reversibility
* confidence
* cost
* expected benefit

Evaluation must be explainable.

---

# Evaluation Registry

Contains:

* evaluation identifier
* option scores
* evaluation criteria
* confidence
* validation history

---

# Layer 4 — Intent Layer

## Purpose

Select the preferred option.

Intent represents the chosen course of action.

Intent is not execution.

Intent is a decision output.

---

# Intent Registry

Contains:

* intent identifier
* selected option
* rationale
* confidence
* governance requirements

Intent is the authoritative output of Decision.

---

# Decision Lifecycle

The lifecycle is:

Situation

↓

Options

↓

Evaluation

↓

Intent

↓

Governance Review

↓

Execution

Decision ends before execution begins.

# Decision Explainability

Every decision must answer:

What was observed?

What options existed?

Why was this option selected?

Why were alternatives rejected?

Explainability is mandatory.

---

# Decision Confidence

Every decision contains confidence.

Confidence derives from:

* DNA quality
* understanding quality
* experience quality
* risk certainty

Confidence does not replace governance.

---

# Decision and DNA

DNA provides facts.

---

# Decision and World Model

The World Model provides understanding.

Decision does not own understanding.

---

# Decision and Experience

Experience provides lessons.

Decision uses lessons to improve choices.

Decision does not own wisdom.

# Decision and Risk

Risk provides severity assessment.

Decision evaluates acceptable actions.

Risk informs decisions.

Risk does not make decisions.

---

# Decision and Governance

Governance evaluates authority.

Decision evaluates desirability.

Governance authorizes.

These responsibilities remain separate.

---

# Decision and Execution

Execution performs actions.

Execution may never replace Decision.

Decision may never replace Execution.

---

# Decision and Cambium
