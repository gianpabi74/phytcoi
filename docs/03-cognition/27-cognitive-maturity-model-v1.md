Proposed

# Purpose

The Cognitive Maturity Model defines how PHYTCOI evaluates its own cognitive development.

Maturity is not intelligence.

Maturity is not autonomy.

Maturity is not authority.

Maturity represents the degree to which cognition has become coherent, assimilated, connected and self-consistent.

The model answers:

> How cognitively developed is the organism?

---

# Relationship With Lifecycle

Cognitive maturity and lifecycle are independent concepts.

Example:

A cognitively mature organism may still remain in OBSERVE lifecycle.

Likewise, an immature organism must never progress to execution-oriented lifecycles.

Lifecycle determines allowed behavior.

Maturity determines cognitive development.

# Maturity Dimensions

The model evaluates six dimensions.

---

## Cognitive Coverage

Question:

> How much cognition exists?

Measurements:

* active organs
* assimilated organs
* cognitive outputs

Purpose:

Measure cognitive breadth.

---

## Cognitive Connectivity

Question:

> Are organs connected?

Measurements:

* dependency integrity
* downstream consumers
* cognitive flow continuity

Purpose:

Measure cognitive integration.

---

## Cognitive Assimilation

Question:

> Has cognition been assimilated?

Measurements:

* self assimilation
* world model assimilation
* observatory visibility

Purpose:

Measure cognitive awareness.

---

## Cognitive Stability

Question:

> Does cognition remain stable across time?

Measurements:

* experience stability
* hypothesis stability
* repeated understanding

Purpose:

Measure cognitive consistency.

---

## Cognitive Coherence

Question:

> Does cognition agree with itself?

Measurements:

* relationship support
* truth support
* understanding support
* world model coherence

Purpose:

Measure internal consistency.

---

## Cognitive Self-Awareness

Question:

> Does the organism understand itself?

Measurements:

* self model
* self reflection
* cognitive identity
* self consistency

Purpose:

Measure introspective capability.

# Maturity Stages

---

## Stage 0 — Seed

Characteristics:

```text
identity only
minimal observation
no assimilation
```

Capabilities:

```text
observe
```

---

## Stage 1 — Sprout

Characteristics:

```text
observer active
discovery active
memory active
```

Capabilities:

```text
observe
discover
remember
```

---

## Stage 2 — Seedling

Characteristics:

```text
experience active
hypothesis active
```

Capabilities:

```text
observe
discover
remember
experience
hypothesize
```

---

## Stage 3 — Early Cognition

Characteristics:

```text
knowledge active
relationship active
truth active
```

Capabilities:

```text
understand structures
derive truths
build cognition
```

---

## Stage 4 — Cognitive Organism

Characteristics:

```text
understanding active
world model active
self assimilation active
```

Capabilities:

```text
synthesize cognition
maintain world model
maintain self model
```

---

## Stage 5 — Pre Autonomous

Characteristics:

```text
full cognitive pipeline
high coherence
high assimilation
high stability
```

Capabilities:

```text
ready for future governance evaluation
```

This stage still does not permit execution.

# Maturity Scoring

The model computes:

```text
coverage_score

connectivity_score

assimilation_score

stability_score

coherence_score

self_awareness_score
```

Combined result:

```text
cognitive_maturity_score
```

Range:

```text
0-100
```

---

# Maturity Constraints

High maturity never grants:

```text
authority

approval

execution

planner activation

habitat modification
```

These require independent governance systems.

# Expected Outcome

The Cognitive Maturity Model provides a measurable representation of cognitive development.

It allows PHYTCOI to understand:

* how much cognition exists
* how coherent cognition is
* how stable cognition is
* how self-aware cognition is

without granting any additional authority or autonomy.
