# Cognitive Integrity Model V1

## Status

Proposed

---

# Purpose

The Cognitive Integrity Model defines how PHYTCOI evaluates the overall health of its cognitive system.

While the Cognitive Anatomy Model describes what exists and the Cognitive Audit Model evaluates individual organs, the Cognitive Integrity Model evaluates the organism as a whole.

It answers:

> Is the cognitive organism functioning as a coherent living system?

---

# Integrity Definition

Cognitive Integrity is the degree to which all cognitive organs operate together as a unified organism.

Integrity is not intelligence.

Integrity is not maturity.

Integrity is not autonomy.

Integrity is the measure of systemic coherence.

---

# Integrity Objectives

The model evaluates whether:

* organs exist
* organs metabolize
* organs are connected
* organs are assimilated
* cognition flows correctly
* cognition remains coherent

---

# Integrity Layers

The model evaluates five layers.

---

## Structural Integrity

Question:

> Does the anatomy exist?

Verification:

```text
organ descriptors
organ states
organ directories
dependency graph
```

Failure examples:

```text
missing organ

missing descriptor

broken anatomy
```

---

## Metabolic Integrity

Question:

> Are organs alive?

Verification:

```text
records generated

state updated

metabolism executed
```

Failure examples:

```text
inactive organ

stalled metabolism

dead cognition
```

---

## Assimilation Integrity

Question:

> Has cognition been assimilated?

Verification:

```text
self model

world model

observatory
```

Failure examples:

```text
unassimilated organ

hidden cognition

isolated cognition
```

---

## Flow Integrity

Question:

> Does cognition move through the organism?

Expected flow:

```text
observer
↓
discovery
↓
memory
↓
experience
↓
hypothesis
↓
knowledge
↓
relationship
↓
truth
↓
understanding
↓
world_model
```

Failure examples:

```text
broken dependency

orphan cognition

flow interruption
```

---

## Coherence Integrity

Question:

> Does cognition agree with itself?

Verification:

```text
truth consistency

relationship consistency

understanding consistency

world model coherence
```

Failure examples:

```text
contradictions

incoherent world model

invalid cognitive chains
```

---

# Integrity States

The organism receives one integrity classification.

---

## HEALTHY

Conditions:

```text
all major layers valid
```

Meaning:

The organism functions coherently.

---

## DEGRADED

Conditions:

```text
minor failures
partial assimilation
limited disconnects
```

Meaning:

The organism remains functional but requires evolution.

---

## FRAGMENTED

Conditions:

```text
multiple disconnected organs
broken cognitive flow
```

Meaning:

The organism possesses cognition but lacks systemic integrity.

---

## CRITICAL

Conditions:

```text
major cognitive failures
loss of coherence
```

Meaning:

Cognition can no longer be trusted.

---

# Integrity Score

The model computes:

```text
structural_score

metabolic_score

assimilation_score

flow_score

coherence_score
```

Combined:

```text
cognitive_integrity_score
```

Range:

```text
0-100
```

---

# Relationship With Maturity

Maturity and Integrity are separate.

Example:

```text
high maturity
low integrity
```

is possible when advanced cognition exists but remains fragmented.

Likewise:

```text
low maturity
high integrity
```

is possible when a simple organism remains fully coherent.

---

# Relationship With Lifecycle

Integrity never changes lifecycle.

The model only evaluates health.

It does not authorize:

```text
planning

governance

execution

habitat modification
```

---

# Expected Outcome

The Cognitive Integrity Model provides a single systemic health assessment for the entire PHYTCOI cognitive organism.

It determines whether cognition functions as:

```text
a collection of organs
```

or

```text
a coherent living cognitive system
```

and becomes the final evaluation layer before future cognitive expansion.
