# Purpose

This document defines Autonomous Execution inside PHYTCOI.

Autonomous Execution is the capability through which the organism performs approved actions without requiring direct human intervention while remaining fully constrained by Governance, Safety, Verification and Habitat First principles.

Autonomy does not mean freedom.

Autonomy means trusted operation within defined boundaries.

The purpose of Autonomous Execution is to allow PHYTCOI to preserve habitat stability and continuity even when no human operator is present.

---

# Why Autonomous Execution Exists

Habitats operate continuously.

Humans do not.

Failures may occur:

during the night

during travel

during absence

during communication loss

An organism responsible for habitat continuity must be capable of acting when human intervention is unavailable.

Autonomous Execution exists to preserve continuity under controlled conditions.

---

Autonomy emerges from:

Autonomy should expand slowly and contract quickly when risk increases.

# What Autonomous Execution Is

Autonomous Execution means:

known recovery paths may execute automatically

known verification paths may execute automatically

---

# What Autonomous Execution Is Not

Autonomous Execution is not:

policy creation

authority creation

governance replacement

unbounded optimization

unrestricted habitat modification

These behaviors are forbidden.

# Trust Foundation

Autonomous Execution depends on trust.

Trust is produced by Governance.

Trust is not produced by the Executor.

The Executor consumes trust.

The Executor does not define trust.
