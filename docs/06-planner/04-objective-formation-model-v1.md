# Purpose

This document defines how objectives are formed inside PHYTCOI.

Objective Formation is the process through which the organism transforms observations, truths, risks and needs into planning goals.

Objectives are the foundation of planning.

Without objectives, planning has no direction.

Without cognition, objectives have no meaning.

Without governance, objectives have no constraints.

Objective Formation exists to create meaningful planning intent while preserving Habitat First principles.

---

# Why Objectives Exist

Observation identifies reality.

Understanding explains reality.

Truth validates reality.

Risk evaluates reality.

None of these define:

```text
What should be pursued?
```

Objectives exist to answer that question.

An objective defines a desired future condition.

Objectives provide direction without prescribing implementation.

---

# Position Inside PHYTCOI

Objective Formation sits between cognition and planning.

The flow is:

Evidence
↓
Understanding
↓
Truth
↓
Risk
↓
Need Detection
↓
Objective Formation
↓
Planning

Objectives transform needs into planning targets.

---

# Definition of an Objective

An objective is a desired future state that the organism wishes to preserve, improve, restore or understand.

Objectives are not actions.

Objectives are not plans.

Objectives are not approvals.

Objectives are directional cognitive constructs.

Example:

```text
Maintain identity continuity.
```

This is an objective.

It does not specify how.

---

# Objective Characteristics

Every objective should be:

purpose-driven

evidence-supported

risk-aware

truth-aware

explainable

auditable

Objectives should remain stable enough to guide planning while remaining adaptable to new evidence.

---

# Objective Lifecycle

Objectives evolve through stages:

Observation
↓
Need Detection
↓
Objective Candidate
↓
Objective Validation
↓
Active Objective
↓
Objective Completion
↓
Objective Retirement

Not every candidate becomes active.

---

# Need Detection

Objective formation begins with need detection.

Needs may emerge from:

Observed instability

Observed uncertainty

Observed degradation

Observed risk

Observed dependency

Observed knowledge gaps

Need detection is not planning.

Need detection only identifies potential motivation.

---

# Objective Candidate Generation

When a need is detected, PHYTCOI may generate one or more objective candidates.

Examples:

```text
Preserve continuity

Increase understanding

Reduce uncertainty

Improve resilience

Protect habitat
```

Multiple candidates should be preferred over single interpretations.

---

# Objective Validation

Objective candidates require validation.

Validation evaluates:

supporting evidence

supporting truths

supporting risks

consistency

relevance

scope

Invalid objectives should be discarded.

---

# Objective Categories

Future objectives may belong to categories such as:

Continuity Objectives

Protection Objectives

Recovery Objectives

Knowledge Objectives

Observation Objectives

Resilience Objectives

Optimization Objectives

Categories improve planner organization.

---

# Continuity Objectives

Purpose:

Preserve ongoing habitat functions.

Examples:

```text
Maintain identity continuity

Maintain resolution continuity

Maintain availability continuity
```

Continuity objectives usually emerge from dependency awareness.

---

# Protection Objectives

Purpose:

Protect habitat capabilities from degradation.

Examples:

```text
Protect critical dependencies

Protect recovery capability

Protect core habitat functions
```

Protection objectives are strongly aligned with Habitat First.

---

# Recovery Objectives

Purpose:

Preserve the ability to recover from failure.

Examples:

```text
Maintain recovery capability

Preserve restoration capability

Preserve rollback capability
```

Recovery objectives increase resilience.

---

# Knowledge Objectives

Purpose:

Reduce uncertainty.

Examples:

```text
Increase domain understanding

Increase capability understanding

Reduce unknown relationships
```

Knowledge objectives dominate early lifecycle stages.

---

# Observation Objectives

Purpose:

Expand organism awareness.

Examples:

```text
Observe additional evidence

Observe temporal stability

Observe dependency evolution
```

Observation objectives are common during OBSERVE.

---

# Resilience Objectives

Purpose:

Increase habitat survivability.

Examples:

```text
Reduce dependency concentration

Improve redundancy awareness

Increase recovery confidence
```

Resilience objectives support long-term survival.

---

# Optimization Objectives

Purpose:

Improve efficiency without reducing safety.

Examples:

```text
Reduce waste

Reduce complexity

Improve resource utilization
```

Optimization must remain subordinate to survival.

---

# Objective Priority

Not all objectives are equal.

Priority should consider:

Habitat impact

Risk impact

Truth confidence

Dependency scope

Temporal stability

Habitat safety always dominates optimization.

---

# Habitat First Objective Rule

When objectives conflict:

Example:

Objective A

```text
Increase efficiency
```

Objective B

```text
Preserve habitat stability
```

PHYTCOI must prefer Objective B.

Habitat preservation overrides optimization.

---

# Objective Awareness

Future implementations should maintain awareness of:

Active objectives

Pending objectives

Completed objectives

Expired objectives

Conflicting objectives

Objective awareness improves explainability.

# Objective Persistence

Objectives should be persisted.

Potential fields:

category

status

priority

supporting_truths

supporting_risks

creation_timestamp

last_review_timestamp

Objectives become part of long-term cognition.

---

# Objective Expiration

Objectives may expire when:

Need disappears

Truth changes

Risk changes

Environment changes

Lifecycle changes

Expired objectives should not remain active.

---

# Objective Revision

Objectives may evolve.

Revision may occur when:

New evidence appears

New truths emerge

Risk changes

Dependency structure changes

Revision is expected and healthy.

---

# Objective Conflicts

Conflicts occur when objectives compete.

Examples:

```text
Optimize performance

vs

Minimize risk
```

```text
Reduce redundancy

vs

Increase resilience
```

Governance should resolve objective conflicts.

---

# Objective Explainability

Every objective should answer:

Why does this objective exist?

Which need created it?

Which truths support it?

Which risks support it?

Which evidence supports it?

Unexplainable objectives are invalid.

---

# Relationship With Planning

Objectives guide planning.

Planning consumes objectives.

Objectives do not contain plans.

One objective may generate multiple plans.

---

# Relationship With Governance

Objectives do not grant authority.

Objectives do not grant approval.

Objectives remain subordinate to governance.

---

# Relationship With Experience

Future implementations should allow experience to influence objective quality.

Repeated successful objectives may become stronger.

Repeated failed objectives may become weaker.

Experience supports maturation.

---

# Failure Modes

Potential failures include:

False objectives

Overfitting objectives

Conflicting objectives

Optimization obsession

Ignoring habitat constraints

Objectives without evidence

The organism must defend against these failures.

# Summary

Objective Formation transforms observed needs into planning goals.
