# PHYTCOI Species Glossary v1

## Purpose

The Species Glossary defines the official vocabulary of PHYTCOI.

If a term is defined here, all future documentation, code, policies and specifications must use the same meaning.

The glossary exists to prevent ambiguity and architectural drift.

---

# Species

The highest biological entity.

PHYTCOI is a species.

A species may contain multiple individuals.

---

# Individual

A living instance of the species.

Examples:

* PHYT-001
* PHYT-002
* PHYT-003

---

# Seed

The reproductive unit of the species.

A Seed contains the minimum inheritance required to create a new individual.

---

# Genome

The universal inheritance of the species.

Shared across all individuals.

Contains species-level knowledge.

---

# DNA

Habitat-specific knowledge generated by an individual.

DNA belongs to the individual.

DNA does not belong to the species.

---

# Habitat

The ecosystem observed and governed by an individual.

The world in which the individual exists.

---

# Habitat Substrate

The foundational platform that sustains the habitat.

Examples:

* hypervisors
* cloud platforms
* physical hosts
* compute fabrics

Function matters.

Vendor names do not.

---

# Nest

The runtime environment hosting an individual.

The body of the individual.

Examples:

* virtual machine
* container
* physical runtime

---

# Organ

A major biological structure responsible for a primary function.

Examples:

* Kernel
* Root System
* Brain System
* Metabolism

An Organ may contain multiple Suborgans.

---

# Suborgan

A structural component belonging to an Organ.

Examples:

* Identity Core
* Planning Engine
* Event Router

---

# Intelligence

A cognitive function.

Intelligences think.

Organs host Intelligences.

Examples:

* Discovery Intelligence
* Decision Intelligence
* Learning Intelligence

---

# Capability

A universal ability.

Capabilities enable actions.

Capabilities are implemented through local adaptations.

---

# Action

A concrete operation performed in reality.

Actions modify or interact with the habitat.

---

# Evidence

Validated observation.

Evidence is the foundation of knowledge.

---

# Knowledge

Evidence-supported understanding.

Knowledge must always be traceable to evidence.

---

# Trust

Confidence in understanding, capabilities or actions.

Trust answers:

"How certain am I?"

---

# Risk

Potential harm.

Risk answers:

"What happens if I am wrong?"

---

# Policy

A rule that constrains behavior.

Policies determine what may or may not occur.

---

# Mission

The purpose of the species.

Mission belongs to the Genome.

---

# Germination

The process that transforms a Seed into a living individual.

---

# Adaptation

A habitat-specific structure generated after germination.

---

# germ_*

Prefix used for generated habitat-specific artifacts.

Examples:

* germ_driver
* germ_capability
* germ_policy
* germ_adapter

---

# Species Memory

Knowledge shared across generations.

Belongs to the species.

---

# Experience Memory

Knowledge generated through lived experience.

Belongs to the individual.

---

# Governance

The process of understanding, protecting and improving a habitat.

Governance exists to preserve continuity.

---

# Steward

The operational role of a PHYTCOI individual.

PHYTCOI is not a ruler.

PHYTCOI is a steward.

---

# Final Rule

If a term is defined in this glossary, all future documentation must use the glossary definition.

Alternative meanings are forbidden.
