# PHYTCOI DNA Model v1

## Purpose

The DNA Model defines how a PHYTCOI individual represents validated knowledge about its habitat.

DNA is the authoritative representation of habitat reality.

DNA does not contain assumptions.

DNA does not contain hypotheses.

DNA does not contain interpretations.

DNA contains validated habitat truth.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Genome defines the species.

Self Model defines the individual.

DNA defines the habitat.

World Model defines the understanding of the habitat.

These domains must remain separate.

---

# Core Question

The DNA answers:

What exists?

The DNA does not answer:

Why does it exist?

What does it mean?

What may happen?

These questions belong to the World Model.

---

# Definition

DNA is the validated structural representation of a habitat.

DNA contains:

* objects
* capabilities
* relationships
* dependencies
* classifications
* evidence references

DNA is continuously updated through observation and validation.

DNA evolves with the habitat.

---

# DNA Ownership

Owner:

DNA System

Primary Contributors:

* Discovery Intelligence
* Evidence Intelligence
* Classification Intelligence
* Validation Intelligence

Consumers:

* World Model
* Decision Intelligence
* Adaptation Intelligence
* Governance Systems
* Cambium

---

# DNA Formation

DNA formation follows a strict chain.

Unknown

↓

Observation

↓

Hypothesis

↓

Validation

↓

DNA

No object may enter DNA without validation.

---

# DNA Purity Principle

DNA must contain only validated knowledge.

The following are forbidden:

* assumptions
* guesses
* beliefs
* unsupported classifications
* speculative relationships

DNA purity is mandatory.

---

# DNA Domains

The DNA is composed of domains.

---

## Object Domain

Answers:

What exists?

Contains:

* object identifier
* object class
* object status
* object ownership
* object evidence

Examples:

* service provider
* identity provider
* name resolution provider
* storage provider
* monitoring provider

Objects are classified by function.

Not by vendor.

---

## Capability Domain

Answers:

What can it do?

Contains:

* provided capabilities
* consumed capabilities
* operational capabilities
* governance capabilities

Capabilities are primary.

Products are secondary.

---

## Relationship Domain

Answers:

How is it connected?

Contains:

* communication relationships
* service relationships
* trust relationships
* operational relationships

Relationships must be evidence-backed.

---

## Dependency Domain

Answers:

What does it require?

Contains:

* direct dependencies
* indirect dependencies
* critical dependencies
* survival dependencies

Dependencies are fundamental to governance.

---

## Classification Domain

Answers:

What is it?

Contains:

* functional classification
* habitat role
* operational role
* criticality classification

Classification must be evidence-based.

---

## Evidence Domain

Answers:

Why do we believe this?

Contains:

* source observations
* validation records
* supporting evidence
* confidence history

Every DNA entry must reference evidence.

---

# Functional Classification Principle

Objects must be classified by function.

Examples:

Correct:

Identity Provider

Incorrect:

Windows Server

Correct:

Name Resolution Provider

Incorrect:

Pi-hole

Correct:

Hosting Provider

Incorrect:

Proxmox

Vendor names may exist as metadata.

They must never be primary classification.

---

# DNA Graph

The DNA is not a list.

The DNA is a graph.

Objects are nodes.

Capabilities are attributes.

Relationships are edges.

Dependencies are directed links.

The graph represents habitat structure.

---

# DNA Confidence

Every DNA entry must contain confidence.

Examples:

95%

Validated

75%

Needs further validation

100%

Confirmed through multiple evidence sources

Confidence must be visible.

---

# DNA Mutation

DNA may evolve.

Valid mutations include:

* object discovery
* object removal
* capability change
* relationship change
* dependency change

All mutations require evidence.

---

# DNA Aging

Knowledge becomes stale.

DNA entries must be periodically revalidated.

Revalidation frequency depends on:

* criticality
* volatility
* trust level

Stale DNA increases governance risk.

---

# DNA Regeneration

DNA must be rebuildable.

A PHYTCOI individual must be capable of rebuilding DNA from:

* observations
* evidence
* validation history

DNA must never become irreplaceable.

---

# DNA Corruption

Corruption occurs when:

* unsupported knowledge exists
* evidence is missing
* relationships are invalid
* classifications are incorrect

Corrupted DNA is a critical condition.

---

# DNA Repair

Repair mechanisms include:

* re-observation
* re-validation
* contradiction analysis
* confidence recalculation
* relationship verification

Repair restores habitat truth.

---

# DNA and Self Model

The Self Model describes:

The individual.

The DNA describes:

The habitat.

The individual must never confuse itself with the habitat.

---

# DNA and World Model

DNA contains facts.

World Model contains understanding.

Example:

DNA:

Object A depends on Object B.

World Model:

Failure of B may disable A.

DNA provides truth.

World Model provides meaning.

---

# DNA and Adaptation

DNA does not create adaptations.

DNA provides the knowledge required to generate adaptations.

Adaptations belong to Cambium.

DNA informs.

Cambium transforms.

---

# DNA and Species Continuity

DNA belongs to the individual.

DNA is not inherited by the species.

Two individuals may share the same Genome.

Two individuals may have different DNA.

Species continuity depends on Genome.

Habitat continuity depends on DNA.

---

# DNA Quality Metrics

Every individual must track:

* object coverage
* capability coverage
* relationship coverage
* dependency coverage
* confidence distribution
* validation freshness

DNA quality must be measurable.

---

# Final Rule

DNA is the validated habitat truth of a PHYTCOI individual.

It answers:

What exists?

It does not answer:

Why?

How?

What if?

Those questions belong to higher cognitive systems.

DNA is the foundation upon which understanding is built.

Without DNA there is no reliable understanding.

Without understanding there is no reliable governance.
