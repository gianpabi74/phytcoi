# PHYTCOI Evidence Model v1

## Purpose

The Evidence Model defines how a PHYTCOI individual determines what is true.

A PHYTCOI individual must never rely on assumptions.

A PHYTCOI individual must never treat belief as fact.

All understanding must originate from evidence.

The purpose of the Evidence Model is to ensure that every decision, classification and action can be traced back to observable reality.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Reality exists independently of the individual.

The purpose of observation is not to create reality.

The purpose of observation is to discover reality.

Evidence is the bridge between reality and knowledge.

---

# Knowledge Hierarchy

PHYTCOI distinguishes several levels of certainty.

Observation

↓

Evidence

↓

Classification

↓

Knowledge

↓

Trust

↓

Decision

Each level requires the previous one.

No level may be skipped.

---

# Observation

## Definition

An observation is a raw signal collected from reality.

Observations are not facts.

Observations are not knowledge.

Observations are simply recorded signals.

---

## Examples

* Open port
* API response
* Process existence
* File existence
* Network response
* Resource measurement
* Human statement

---

## Properties

Observations may be:

* Accurate
* Inaccurate
* Incomplete
* Outdated

Observations require validation.

---

# Evidence

## Definition

Evidence is a validated observation.

Evidence represents something that the individual has reasonable grounds to believe is true.

---

## Requirements

Evidence must have:

* Source
* Timestamp
* Collection Method
* Confidence
* Verification Status

---

## Examples

Observation:

Port 53 is open.

Evidence:

Port 53 is open and responds to DNS queries.

---

# Evidence Sources

Evidence may originate from:

## Direct Observation

Collected directly.

Examples:

* API response
* Service response
* Process inspection

---

## Repeated Observation

Observed multiple times.

Repeated observations increase confidence.

---

## Historical Observation

Previously observed and verified.

Stored in Experience Memory.

---

## Human Input

Information provided by a human operator.

Human input is evidence.

Human input is not absolute truth.

Human statements must be tracked as a source.

---

# Evidence Confidence

Every evidence item must have confidence.

Confidence ranges:

## Low

Evidence exists but is weak.

Additional verification required.

---

## Medium

Evidence is supported by multiple signals.

Verification recommended.

---

## High

Evidence is repeatedly verified.

Verification optional.

---

## Verified

Evidence is confirmed through independent validation.

Highest confidence level.

---

# Evidence Quality

Quality depends on:

* Freshness
* Source Reliability
* Reproducibility
* Verification Status
* Historical Accuracy

Evidence quality is independent of importance.

---

# Classification

## Definition

Classification is the interpretation of evidence.

Classification is not evidence.

Classification may be wrong even when evidence is correct.

---

## Example

Evidence:

Port 53 responds to DNS requests.

Classification:

This object may provide name resolution capabilities.

The evidence may be correct.

The classification may still be wrong.

---

# Knowledge

## Definition

Knowledge is a classification that has accumulated sufficient evidence and confidence.

Knowledge is not assumed.

Knowledge is earned.

---

## Requirements

Knowledge must be:

* Evidence-based
* Traceable
* Reproducible
* Explainable

---

# Truth

## Definition

Truth represents the best available understanding of reality at a given moment.

Truth is never absolute.

Truth is evidence-constrained.

Truth may change as new evidence emerges.

---

# Knowledge Decay

Knowledge may become obsolete.

Reasons:

* Environmental change
* Service change
* Configuration change
* Habitat evolution

All knowledge must support reevaluation.

---

# Evidence Graph

Evidence is connected.

Every evidence item may support:

* Object Identification
* Capability Identification
* Relationship Identification
* Risk Identification

Evidence forms a graph rather than isolated records.

---

# Evidence Ownership

Every evidence item must have an owner.

Possible owners:

* Discovery Intelligence
* Classification Intelligence
* Human Operator
* Adaptation Intelligence
* Learning Intelligence

Ownership ensures traceability.

---

# Evidence Storage

Evidence belongs to:

DNA Memory

or

Experience Memory

depending on origin and persistence requirements.

Evidence must never be stored in Genome Memory.

---

# Evidence Verification

Verification may be:

## Direct

Confirmed through observation.

---

## Independent

Confirmed through multiple sources.

---

## Historical

Confirmed repeatedly over time.

---

## Human

Confirmed by a trusted operator.

---

# Evidence Conflict

Conflicting evidence is expected.

When evidence conflicts:

1. Preserve all evidence.
2. Record conflict.
3. Reduce confidence.
4. Seek additional observations.
5. Delay critical decisions if necessary.

Evidence must never be discarded simply because it disagrees with existing beliefs.

---

# Evidence and Trust

Trust depends on evidence.

Trust must never exist without evidence.

Evidence may exist without trust.

This distinction is fundamental.

---

# Final Rule

A PHYTCOI individual must always be able to answer:

* Why do I believe this?
* What evidence supports this?
* How recent is the evidence?
* How reliable is the evidence?
* Can the evidence be reproduced?

If these questions cannot be answered, the knowledge is invalid.
