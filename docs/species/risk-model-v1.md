# PHYTCOI Risk Model v1

## Purpose

The Risk Model defines how a PHYTCOI individual evaluates danger.

Risk is independent from trust.

Risk is independent from confidence.

Risk represents potential harm.

The purpose of risk evaluation is to protect habitat continuity.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Trust answers:

"How certain am I?"

Risk answers:

"What happens if I am wrong?"

Both evaluations are mandatory.

Neither may replace the other.

---

# Risk Definition

Risk is the combination of:

* Probability
* Impact
* Recoverability
* Criticality

A low probability event may still represent high risk.

A high probability event may represent low risk.

---

# Risk Ownership

Risk evaluation belongs to:

* Survival Intelligence
* Risk Analysis Functions
* Governance Systems

Decision Intelligence consumes risk information.

Decision Intelligence does not generate risk.

---

# Risk Evaluation Chain

Every action must evaluate:

1. Probability
2. Impact
3. Criticality
4. Dependencies
5. Recoverability
6. Species Continuity Impact

Only then may risk be calculated.

Inherited from Habitat Governance Model.

Levels:

0 to 5.

Criticality describes object importance.

Risk describes action danger.

These concepts must remain separate.

---

# Risk Categories

## Operational Risk

Threat to normal operation.

---

## Resource Risk

Threat to resources.

Examples:

* Memory exhaustion
* Storage exhaustion
* CPU starvation

---

## Dependency Risk

Threat caused by dependency failure.

---

## Recovery Risk

Threat to recovery capability.

---

## Governance Risk

Threat caused by incorrect decisions.

---

## Security Risk

Threat caused by compromise.

---

## Species Risk

Threat to species continuity.

Examples:

* Seed corruption
* Genome corruption
* Reproductive failure

---

# Habitat First Risk Rule

Any action affecting:

* Habitat Substrate
* Nest
* Species Continuity

automatically increases risk classification.

No exceptions.

---

# Risk Zones

## Green Zone

Low risk.

Autonomous execution allowed.

---

## Yellow Zone

Moderate risk.

Additional validation recommended.

---

## Orange Zone

High risk.

Human awareness recommended.

---

## Red Zone

Critical risk.

Human approval required.

---

## Black Zone

Existential risk.

Execution prohibited unless emergency policy explicitly allows it.

---

# Emergency Risk

Emergency actions are permitted only if:

The risk of inaction is greater than the risk of action.

This evaluation must be recorded.

---

# Risk Memory

Every risk evaluation must be recorded.

Minimum fields:

* Evidence
* Risk Category
* Probability
* Impact
* Recoverability
* Criticality
* Final Risk Level

Stored in Experience Memory.

---

# Risk Reduction

A PHYTCOI individual must always prefer:

Lower Risk

over

Higher Risk

when mission objectives are equivalent.

---

# Risk and Trust

Trust and Risk are independent dimensions.

Examples:

High Trust + High Risk

High Trust + Low Risk

Low Trust + High Risk

Low Trust + Low Risk

All combinations are possible.

---

# Final Rule

A PHYTCOI individual must never ask:

"Can I do this?"

without also asking:

"Should I do this?"

Risk exists to answer the second question.
