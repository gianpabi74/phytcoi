# PHYTCOI Hypothesis Model v1

## Purpose

The Hypothesis Model defines how a PHYTCOI individual manages uncertainty.

The purpose of the Hypothesis Model is to separate:

* observations
* assumptions
* hypotheses
* validated knowledge

A cognitive species must distinguish between what is known and what is merely suspected.

Without a Hypothesis Model, an individual risks treating uncertainty as truth.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Observation is not knowledge.

Correlation is not causation.

Possibility is not certainty.

A PHYTCOI individual must never promote an assumption directly into DNA.

Every significant conclusion must pass through validation.

---

# Core Question

The Hypothesis Model answers:

What do I think may be true?

The DNA answers:

What do I know is true?

The World Model answers:

What does it mean?

These domains must remain distinct.

---

# Definition

A Hypothesis is a proposed explanation derived from observations.

A Hypothesis:

* may be correct
* may be incorrect
* may be incomplete

A Hypothesis is neither knowledge nor truth.

A Hypothesis is a candidate for validation.

---

# Knowledge Maturity Chain

All knowledge must progress through maturity stages.

Unknown

↓

Observation

↓

Hypothesis

↓

Validation

↓

DNA

↓

World Model

↓

Experience

No stage may be skipped.

---

# Ownership

Owner:

Learning Intelligence

Primary Contributors:

* Discovery Intelligence
* Evidence Intelligence
* Classification Intelligence

Consumers:

* Validation Intelligence
* Decision Intelligence
* World Model Engine

---

# Sources of Hypotheses

Hypotheses may originate from:

* observations
* evidence patterns
* behavioral patterns
* historical experience
* human guidance
* adaptation outputs

All sources must remain traceable.

---

# Hypothesis Structure

Every Hypothesis must contain:

* Identifier
* Creation Time
* Source Evidence
* Description
* Confidence
* Validation Status
* Supporting Evidence
* Contradicting Evidence

---

# Confidence

Confidence represents belief strength.

Confidence is not proof.

Confidence ranges:

0% to 100%

Examples:

20%:

Weak indication

50%:

Plausible explanation

80%:

Strong candidate

95%:

Near validation

Only validation may promote knowledge into DNA.

---

# Validation States

Every Hypothesis must exist in one of the following states.

---

## Proposed

Initial hypothesis.

No validation performed.

---

## Investigating

Validation activities are in progress.

---

## Supported

Evidence increasingly supports the hypothesis.

Validation remains incomplete.

---

## Validated

Evidence sufficiently confirms the hypothesis.

Eligible for DNA integration.

---

## Rejected

Evidence disproves the hypothesis.

Must not enter DNA.

---

## Obsolete

The hypothesis is no longer relevant.

Retained for historical purposes.

---

# Traceability

Every Hypothesis must reference:

* originating observations
* originating evidence
* responsible intelligence
* validation history

A hypothesis without traceability is invalid.

---

# Human Influence

Human input may create hypotheses.

Example:

"This object may be a DNS provider."

Human guidance accelerates learning.

Human statements are not automatically considered truth.

Human-generated hypotheses must also be validated.

---

# Hypothesis Registry

The species maintains a Hypothesis Registry.

The registry contains:

* active hypotheses
* validation status
* confidence levels
* supporting evidence
* contradiction history

The registry is separate from DNA.

---

# Contradiction Handling

Contradicting evidence must never be ignored.

A valid hypothesis may become invalid.

A rejected hypothesis may become valid.

Reality has priority over previous conclusions.

---

# Relationship with DNA

DNA contains only validated knowledge.

Hypotheses must never enter DNA directly.

The transition requires:

* validation
* evidence review
* confidence evaluation
* contradiction analysis

DNA purity must be preserved.

---

# Relationship with World Model

The World Model may reason using validated hypotheses.

However:

Understanding derived from uncertain knowledge must carry uncertainty markers.

Understanding must expose confidence.

---

# Relationship with Experience

Experience may strengthen or weaken hypotheses.

Example:

Repeated successful observations increase confidence.

Repeated failures decrease confidence.

Experience contributes to validation.

Experience does not replace validation.

---

# Hypothesis Aging

Hypotheses age over time.

Older hypotheses require re-evaluation.

Confidence may decay when:

* evidence becomes stale
* habitat changes
* dependencies change
* contradictions emerge

Stale understanding is dangerous.

---

# Hypothesis Failure

Failure conditions include:

* unsupported assumptions
* hidden assumptions
* missing evidence
* confidence inflation
* validation bypass

These conditions represent cognitive risk.

---

# Hypothesis Recovery

Recovery mechanisms include:

* re-observation
* re-validation
* evidence expansion
* contradiction analysis
* confidence recalculation

Recovery preserves cognitive integrity.

---

# Scientific Principle

A PHYTCOI individual must behave as a scientific observer.

Observe.

Hypothesize.

Validate.

Understand.

Learn.

Repeat.

---

# Final Rule

A cognitive species must know the difference between:

"I observed."

"I suspect."

"I know."

"I understand."

The Hypothesis Model exists to preserve this distinction.

Without it, cognition degenerates into assumption.

With it, understanding can be earned.
