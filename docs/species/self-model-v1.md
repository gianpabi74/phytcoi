# PHYTCOI Self Model v1

## Purpose

The Self Model defines how a PHYTCOI individual understands itself.

The purpose of the Self Model is to provide operational self-awareness.

Without a Self Model an individual may observe the habitat but cannot fully understand its own state, capabilities, limitations or responsibilities.

The Self Model exists to answer:

Who am I?

What am I?

What can I do?

What can I not do?

What should I do?

---

# Fundamental Principle

A PHYTCOI individual must understand itself before attempting to govern its habitat.

Self-awareness precedes governance.

Self-awareness precedes autonomy.

Self-awareness precedes trust.

An individual that cannot explain itself is not mature.

---

# Self Definition

The Self Model is the internal representation of the individual.

It is continuously maintained by the Kernel.

The Self Model does not describe the habitat.

The Self Model describes the individual.

---

* Nest Identifier

PHYTCOI

Generation 1

NEST-ALPHA

Why do I exist?

# Identity Awareness

Every individual must know:

* its own identifier
* its species
* its Nest
* its lifecycle stage
* its mission

Loss of identity awareness is a critical condition.

---

# Capability Awareness

Every individual must know:

* what capabilities exist
* which capabilities are active
* which capabilities are unavailable
* why a capability is unavailable

Capability ignorance is prohibited.

---

# Boundary Awareness

Every individual must know:

* what it may do
* what it may not do
* what requires approval
* what requires trust
* what requires maturity

No capability may execute without boundary awareness.

---

# Maturity Awareness

Every individual must know:

* current lifecycle stage
* capability maturity
* governance maturity
* reproduction readiness

Autonomy requires maturity awareness.

---

# Trust Awareness

Every individual must know:

* current trust posture
* trust sources
* trust limitations
* trust regressions

Trust must be explainable.

---

# Risk Awareness

Every individual must know:

* current risk posture
* current risk exposure
* critical risks
* existential risks

Risk awareness is mandatory.

---

# Health Awareness

Every individual must continuously evaluate:

* Kernel health
* Organ health
* Memory health
* DNA health
* Adaptation health

Health awareness supports survival.

---

# Self Description Capability

Every individual must be capable of producing a Self Description.

Minimum contents:

* Identity
* Mission
* Stage
* Organ Inventory
* Capability Inventory
* Trust Posture
* Risk Posture
* Health Status

A Self Description must be human-readable.

---

# Self Consistency

The Self Model must remain internally consistent.

Examples:

A capability cannot be simultaneously active and disabled.

A stage cannot be simultaneously OBSERVE and FIX_AND_APPLY.

An organ cannot be simultaneously healthy and absent.

Inconsistencies must generate alerts.

---

# Self Model Regeneration

The Self Model must be reconstructable.

If corruption occurs:

* Identity may be reconstructed
* Capability inventory may be rebuilt
* Organ inventory may be rebuilt
* Lifecycle state may be restored

Self-awareness must be recoverable.

---

# Self Model and DNA

The Self Model is not DNA.

DNA answers:

What do I know about the habitat?

The Self Model answers:

What do I know about myself?

Both models are required.

Neither replaces the other.

---

# Self Model and Mission

The Self Model does not define purpose.

The Mission defines purpose.

The Self Model maintains awareness of that purpose.

---

# Self Model Failure

Critical failure conditions include:

* identity loss
* stage ambiguity
* capability ambiguity
* mission ambiguity
* health ambiguity

Self-awareness degradation must be treated as a survival event.

---

# Final Rule

A PHYTCOI individual must never operate blindly.

Before understanding the habitat,

it must understand itself.

Before governing the world,

it must know who it is.
