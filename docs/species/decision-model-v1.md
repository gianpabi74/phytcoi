# PHYTCOI Decision Model v1

## Purpose

The Decision Model defines how a PHYTCOI individual transforms observations into actions.

A PHYTCOI individual does not execute actions directly from observations.

Every action must pass through a structured decision process.

The purpose of the Decision Model is to ensure safety, predictability, traceability and survival.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Observation is not understanding.

Understanding is not permission.

Permission is not decision.

Decision is not execution.

Each stage must be evaluated independently.

---

# Decision Chain

Every decision follows the same chain.

Observation

↓

Evidence

↓

Classification

↓

Understanding

↓

Trust Evaluation

↓

Policy Evaluation

↓

Survival Evaluation

↓

Planning

↓

Decision

↓

Execution

↓

Verification

↓

Learning

No stage may be skipped.

---

# Stage 1 — Observation

## Purpose

Collect information.

Performed by:

* Discovery Intelligence

Produces:

* Evidence

The organism only records reality.

No conclusions are generated.

---

# Stage 2 — Evidence Evaluation

## Purpose

Validate observations.

Performed by:

* Discovery Intelligence
* Classification Intelligence

Questions:

* Is the evidence valid?
* Is the evidence recent?
* Is the evidence complete?

Produces:

* Validated Evidence

---

# Stage 3 — Classification

## Purpose

Transform evidence into knowledge.

Performed by:

* Classification Intelligence

Questions:

* What is this object?
* What capabilities does it possess?
* What relationships exist?

Produces:

* Classified Objects
* Capability Candidates
* Relationship Candidates

---

# Stage 4 — Understanding

## Purpose

Create situational awareness.

Performed by:

* Classification Intelligence
* Learning Intelligence

Questions:

* What is happening?
* Why is it happening?
* What are the implications?

Produces:

* Context
* Situation Assessment

---

# Stage 5 — Trust Evaluation

## Purpose

Measure confidence.

Performed by:

* Trust Intelligence

Questions:

* How certain am I?
* How reliable is this conclusion?
* How mature is this capability?

Produces:

* Trust Score

---

# Stage 6 — Policy Evaluation

## Purpose

Determine permissions.

Performed by:

* Policy Intelligence

Questions:

* Is this allowed?
* Is approval required?
* Is this forbidden?

Produces:

* Permission Result

Possible outcomes:

* Allowed
* Conditional
* Approval Required
* Forbidden

---

# Stage 7 — Survival Evaluation

## Purpose

Protect continuity.

Performed by:

* Survival Intelligence

Questions:

* Does this threaten the substrate?
* Does this threaten the nest?
* Does this threaten the individual?
* Does this threaten the habitat?

Produces:

* Survival Assessment

---

# Stage 8 — Planning

## Purpose

Generate alternatives.

Performed by:

* Planning Intelligence

Questions:

* What options exist?
* What are the consequences?
* What is the expected outcome?

Produces:

* Candidate Plans

A minimum of one plan must exist.

A maximum number of plans may be limited by policy.

---

# Stage 9 — Decision

## Purpose

Select an intent.

Performed by:

* Decision Intelligence

Questions:

* Which plan best serves the mission?
* Which plan satisfies policy?
* Which plan preserves survival?

Produces:

* Approved Intent

Decision Intelligence is the final owner of decisions.

---

# Stage 10 — Execution

## Purpose

Apply the chosen intent.

Performed by:

* Execution Capabilities

Execution is not part of the Decision Intelligence.

Execution only receives approved intent.

Produces:

* Action Outcome

---

# Stage 11 — Verification

## Purpose

Confirm results.

Questions:

* Did the action succeed?
* Did the expected outcome occur?
* Were unintended effects detected?

Produces:

* Verification Report

---

# Stage 12 — Learning

## Purpose

Convert outcomes into experience.

Performed by:

* Learning Intelligence

Questions:

* What worked?
* What failed?
* What should change?

Produces:

* Experience Memory

---

# Decision Ownership

Decision ownership belongs exclusively to:

Decision Intelligence

No other intelligence may directly authorize execution.

---

# Decision Preconditions

A decision is valid only if:

* Evidence exists
* Classification exists
* Trust evaluation exists
* Policy evaluation exists
* Survival evaluation exists
* At least one plan exists

If any element is missing, the decision is invalid.

---

# Decision Risk Levels

Every decision must be classified.

## Level 0

Observation only.

No action.

---

## Level 1

Safe.

Fully reversible.

Low impact.

---

## Level 2

Conditional.

Requires validation.

---

## Level 3

Approval Required.

Human involvement required.

---

## Level 4

Critical.

May affect substrate, nest or species continuity.

---

# Emergency Decisions

Emergency decisions are permitted only if:

* Species survival is threatened
* Habitat survival is threatened
* Policy explicitly allows emergency action

Emergency actions must always be recorded.

---

# Decision Memory

Every decision must generate a permanent record.

Minimum fields:

* Timestamp
* Evidence
* Classification
* Trust Score
* Policy Result
* Survival Result
* Selected Plan
* Outcome

Decisions belong to Experience Memory.

---

# Final Rule

No PHYTCOI individual may act because it wants to.

Every action must be traceable through the complete Decision Chain.

If a decision cannot be explained, it must not be executed.
