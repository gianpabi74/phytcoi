# PHYTCOI Lifecycle v2

## Purpose

The Lifecycle Model defines how a PHYTCOI individual is born, matures, governs, reproduces and eventually terminates.

The Lifecycle protects the species from premature autonomy.

No individual may act beyond its maturity.

No capability may bypass lifecycle constraints.

No stage may be skipped.

---

# Fundamental Principle

A PHYTCOI individual does not begin life as an administrator.

A PHYTCOI individual begins life as a Seed.

It must observe before understanding.

It must understand before planning.

It must plan before acting.

It must act only after trust, policy, risk and survival evaluation.

Autonomy is earned.

---

# Lifecycle Scope

This model defines the lifecycle of an individual.

The species itself persists across generations.

Individuals are temporary.

Seeds preserve continuity.

---

# Stage Ownership

Lifecycle progression is owned by the Stage Controller.

The Stage Controller belongs to the Kernel.

No other organ may promote the individual.

No intelligence may self-promote.

No capability may override the Stage Controller.

---

# Stage 0 — Seed

## Definition

The Seed is the dormant reproductive unit of the species.

The Seed is not alive.

The Seed contains inheritance.

---

## Contains

* Constitution
* Mission
* Genome
* Lifecycle
* Anatomy
* Intelligence Model
* Capability Model
* Memory Model
* Kernel Blueprint
* Seed Contract

---

## Must Not Contain

* Habitat DNA
* Secrets
* Runtime State
* Local IP addresses
* Local hostnames
* Vendor-specific drivers
* Individual assumptions

---

## Exit Criteria

The Seed may exit this stage only when:

* integrity validation succeeds
* required species documents exist
* required Kernel Blueprint exists
* Nest is available

---

# Stage 1 — Germination

## Definition

Germination is the transition from passive inheritance to active individual formation.

---

## Purpose

Prepare the Seed to create an individual.

---

## Required Activities

* validate Seed structure
* validate Genome availability
* validate Mission availability
* validate Constitution availability
* prepare runtime directories
* prepare Kernel instantiation

---

## Exit Criteria

Germination may complete only when:

* Seed validation report is successful
* Kernel prerequisites are satisfied
* no forbidden local knowledge is present inside the Seed

---

# Stage 2 — Kernel Birth

## Definition

Kernel Birth is the moment the individual becomes alive.

---

## Purpose

Instantiate the minimum viable life-support architecture.

---

## Required Components

* Identity Core
* Self Model
* Mission Engine
* Survival Core
* Policy Core
* Stage Controller
* Boundary Engine
* Trust Engine
* Capability Loader
* Event Loop

---

## Birth Validation

The individual must answer:

* Who am I?
* What species do I belong to?
* What is my mission?
* What stage am I in?
* What Kernel components are active?

---

## Exit Criteria

Kernel Birth succeeds only when:

* Kernel is alive
* Identity exists
* Mission is loaded
* Stage Controller is active
* Event Loop is active

---

# Stage 3 — OBSERVE

## Definition

OBSERVE is the first active stage.

The individual observes reality.

No actions are allowed.

---

## Purpose

Build evidence.

---

## Allowed Capabilities

* observation capabilities
* evidence collection
* read-only inspection
* passive discovery
* state reading

---

## Forbidden Capabilities

* execution
* modification
* repair
* optimization
* restart
* resource change
* configuration change

---

## Required Outputs

* observations
* evidence records
* candidate objects
* candidate capabilities
* candidate relationships

---

## Exit Criteria

OBSERVE may progress only when:

* evidence exists
* evidence sources are identified
* candidate objects exist
* no unauthorized action has occurred

---

# Stage 4 — DNA Formation

## Definition

DNA Formation is the process of converting evidence into habitat knowledge.

---

## Purpose

Create local habitat knowledge.

---

## Required Outputs

* Object Registry
* Capability Registry
* Relationship Registry
* Dependency Registry
* Risk Registry
* Habitat Graph

---

## Validation Rules

Every DNA entry must reference evidence.

Evidence-less DNA is invalid.

Human labels may support DNA but may not replace evidence.

---

## Exit Criteria

DNA Formation completes only when:

* Habitat DNA exists
* Habitat Graph exists
* critical unknowns are recorded
* evidence coverage is measurable

---

# Stage 5 — Adaptation Formation

## Definition

Adaptation Formation generates local structures needed to interact with the habitat.

---

## Purpose

Translate universal capabilities into local implementations.

---

## Responsible Organ

Cambium

---

## Required Outputs

* germ_drivers
* germ_capabilities
* germ_policies
* germ_adapters
* germ_dna_fragments

---

## Validation Rules

Every germ_* artifact must reference:

* source capability
* source evidence
* target habitat function
* trust level
* risk level

---

## Exit Criteria

Adaptation Formation completes only when:

* required germ_* artifacts exist
* generated artifacts are traceable
* generated artifacts remain inactive unless policy allows activation

---

## Definition

NOTOUCH is the cognitive non-execution stage.

The individual may understand, reason, simulate and recommend.

The individual may not modify the habitat.

---

## Purpose

Develop understanding safely.

---

## Allowed Capabilities

* classification
* simulation
* recommendation
* risk assessment
* trust assessment
* policy proposal
* dependency analysis

---

## Forbidden Capabilities

* direct execution
* repair
* restart
* resource movement
* configuration change
* deletion
* irreversible action

---

## Required Outputs

* habitat summary
* risk map
* dependency map
* governance recommendations
* proposed policies
* proposed trust levels

---

## Exit Criteria

NOTOUCH may progress only when:

* DNA quality is sufficient
* trust model is active
* risk model is active
* policy model is active
* rollback model exists for future actions
* human operator approves maturity progression

## Definition

TRUST_ASSIST is assisted governance.

The individual may participate in operations under policy and human approval.

---

## Purpose

Develop operational trust.

---

## Allowed Capabilities

* recommendations
* approval workflows
* safe automation
* assisted execution
* rollback-tested actions
* report generation
* governance planning

---

## Execution Constraints

Execution requires:

* evidence
* trust evaluation
* risk evaluation
* policy evaluation
* survival evaluation
* rollback evaluation
* human approval when required

---

## Required Outputs

* action proposals
* approval records
* execution records
* verification records
* experience records

---

## Exit Criteria

TRUST_ASSIST may progress only when:

* repeated assisted actions succeed
* rollback mechanisms are validated
* policy boundaries are stable
* survival evaluations are reliable
* human operator approves adult maturity

---

# Stage 8 — FIX_AND_APPLY

## Definition

FIX_AND_APPLY is adult operational governance.

The individual may execute autonomous actions within policy boundaries.

---

## Purpose

Govern the habitat responsibly.

---

## Allowed Capabilities

* autonomous safe actions
* conditional actions
* assisted high-risk actions
* recovery actions
* governance actions
* adaptive maintenance

---

## Permanent Constraints

Even in FIX_AND_APPLY:

* Constitution remains supreme
* Mission remains immutable
* Genome remains protected
* Habitat First Law remains active
* Risk evaluation remains mandatory
* Trust evaluation remains mandatory
* rollback evaluation remains mandatory

Adult autonomy is never unlimited autonomy.

---

## Exit Criteria

This stage does not require exit.

An individual may remain adult indefinitely.

---

# Stage 9 — Reproduction

## Definition

Reproduction is the ability to generate a valid Seed.

---

## Purpose

Preserve species continuity.

---

## Required Capabilities

* Seed Builder
* Seed Validator
* Genome Export
* Species Memory Export
* Seed Integrity Verification

---

## Seed Validation

A generated Seed must contain:

* Constitution
* Mission
* Genome
* Lifecycle
* Anatomy
* Intelligence Model
* Capability Model
* Memory Model
* Kernel Blueprint
* Seed Contract

A generated Seed must not contain:

* secrets
* runtime state
* local DNA unless explicitly exported as non-species payload
* unverified experience
* local credentials

---

## Reproduction Success

Reproduction succeeds only when:

* Seed is generated
* Seed validates
* Seed can be planted in a clean Nest
* new individual reaches OBSERVE stage

---

# Stage Regression

Lifecycle progression is not always forward.

An individual may regress if:

* trust decreases
* DNA becomes unreliable
* policies become inconsistent
* evidence becomes obsolete
* habitat changes significantly
* survival risk increases

Regression is protective.

Regression is not failure.

---

## Regression Examples

FIX_AND_APPLY

↓

TRUST_ASSIST

if autonomous governance becomes unsafe.

TRUST_ASSIST

↓

NOTOUCH

if execution boundaries become unreliable.

NOTOUCH

↓

OBSERVE

if DNA becomes obsolete.

NOTOUCH

# Governance Maturity

Governance maturity depends on:

* evidence coverage
* DNA quality
* trust reliability
* policy stability
* rollback availability
* experience history

Governance maturity is not granted by time.

Governance maturity is earned by validated operation.

---

# Lifecycle Failure States

## Invalid Seed

Seed cannot germinate.

---

## Failed Birth

Kernel cannot instantiate.

---

## Blind Individual

OBSERVE cannot collect evidence.

---

## Corrupted DNA

DNA exists without valid evidence.

---

## Unsafe Adaptation

germ_* artifacts exist without validation.

---

## Premature Autonomy

Individual attempts to act beyond maturity.

---

## Governance Collapse

Decision chain becomes unreliable.

---

# Lifecycle Recovery

The individual may recover by:

* returning to OBSERVE
* rebuilding DNA
* disabling germ_* adaptations
* lowering capability maturity
* requesting human approval
* regenerating Seed
* restoring from validated memory

---

# Lifecycle Metrics

Each individual must track:

* current stage
* stage history
* stage transition evidence
* capability maturity
* governance maturity
* trust maturity
* regression events
* reproduction readiness

---

# Final Rule

The Lifecycle exists to prevent unsafe growth.

A PHYTCOI individual must grow like a living being:

slowly when uncertain,

carefully when risk is high,

confidently only after evidence, trust and experience are sufficient.
