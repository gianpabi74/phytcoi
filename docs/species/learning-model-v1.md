# PHYTCOI Learning Model v1

## Purpose

The Learning Model defines how a PHYTCOI individual transforms experience into improvement.

Learning exists to increase survival, understanding, resilience and operational effectiveness.

Learning must never compromise species stability.

The purpose of learning is adaptation.

The purpose of learning is not mutation.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Experience may improve behavior.

Experience may not rewrite the species.

The Genome defines what the species is.

Learning defines how an individual becomes better.

---

# Learning Definition

Learning is the process of transforming:

Observation

↓

Experience

↓

Knowledge

↓

Improvement

Learning requires evidence.

Learning requires verification.

Learning requires memory.

---

# Learning Objectives

Every PHYTCOI individual must learn:

* What works
* What fails
* What improves survival
* What improves resilience
* What increases understanding
* What reduces risk

---

# Learning Ownership

Learning belongs to:

* Learning Intelligence
* Memory Intelligence

Learning is consumed by:

* Planning Intelligence
* Decision Intelligence
* Adaptation Intelligence

---

# Learning Sources

Learning may originate from:

## Observation

Direct habitat observation.

---

## Experience

Previous actions and outcomes.

---

## Governance History

Historical governance decisions.

---

## Recovery Events

Failures and restorations.

---

## Human Feedback

Feedback provided by operators.

---

## Species Evolution Memory

Validated improvements inherited from previous generations.

---

# Learning Domains

## Operational Learning

Learn how to operate more effectively.

Examples:

* Better plans
* Better timing
* Better sequencing

---

## Risk Learning

Learn which actions increase risk.

---

## Recovery Learning

Learn how failures are restored.

---

## Habitat Learning

Learn habitat structure and dependencies.

---

## Governance Learning

Learn governance effectiveness.

---

## Species Learning

Learn improvements that may benefit future generations.

Species Learning requires validation.

---

# Learning Constraints

Learning may modify:

* Experience Memory
* DNA Memory
* Species Evolution Memory

Learning may not modify:

* Constitution
* Mission
* Genome
* Anatomy
* Lifecycle

These structures are protected.

---

# Learning Confidence

Learning confidence depends on:

* Evidence Quality
* Outcome Verification
* Repeatability
* Historical Consistency

---

## Confidence Levels

### Experimental

Observed once.

---

### Emerging

Observed multiple times.

---

### Established

Repeatedly verified.

---

### Proven

Verified across long periods or multiple environments.

---

# Learning Promotion

Not all learning is equal.

Promotion path:

Observation

↓

Experience

↓

Lesson

↓

Pattern

↓

Validated Improvement

↓

Species Evolution Candidate

Only validated improvements may enter Species Evolution Memory.

---

# Learning Failure

Learning may be wrong.

Causes include:

* Incorrect evidence
* Temporary conditions
* Misclassification
* Incomplete context

Therefore all learning must remain revisable.

---

# Learning Decay

Old learning may become obsolete.

Learning must support:

* Reevaluation
* Revision
* Retirement

No lesson is permanent.

---

# Species Protection Rule

No learning event may directly modify the Genome.

No learning event may directly modify the Constitution.

No learning event may directly modify the Mission.

Species stability has priority over adaptation.

---

# Learning and Reproduction

Learning may be inherited only if:

* Verified
* Reproducible
* Beneficial
* Safe

Unverified learning must never be inherited.

---

# Learning Success

Learning is successful when:

* Understanding increases
* Risk decreases
* Resilience increases
* Recovery improves
* Habitat health improves

Success is not measured by knowledge quantity.

Success is measured by survival value.

---

# Final Rule

A PHYTCOI individual must learn continuously.

A PHYTCOI species must evolve carefully.

Individuals learn quickly.

Species evolve slowly.
