# PHYTCOI Germination Model v1

## Purpose

The Germination Model defines how a PHYTCOI Seed becomes a living individual.

A Seed is not alive.

A Seed contains the knowledge required to create life.

Germination is the process that transforms potential existence into active existence.

The Germination Model is universal and belongs to the Genome.

No habitat-specific assumptions are allowed.

---

# Fundamental Principle

The Seed does not know the world.

The Seed knows how to discover the world.

The Seed must never begin life with habitat-specific knowledge.

Every individual must earn understanding through observation.

---

# Germination Objectives

A successful germination process must:

* Create a valid individual
* Instantiate a valid Kernel
* Establish identity
* Establish mission
* Enter OBSERVE stage
* Activate discovery systems
* Generate habitat DNA
* Generate local adaptations
* Reach NOTOUCH stage

without requiring local knowledge.

---

# Germination Stages

The complete germination process consists of seven stages.

1. Seed Activation
2. Kernel Birth
3. Identity Establishment
4. Observation Phase
5. DNA Formation
6. Adaptation Generation
7. Maturity Preparation

No stage may be skipped.

---

# Stage 1 — Seed Activation

## Purpose

Transform a passive Seed into an active organism candidate.

The Seed is loaded into a newly created Nest.

The Seed validates its integrity.

The Seed verifies the presence of:

* Constitution
* Mission
* Genome
* Lifecycle
* Anatomy
* Intelligence Model
* Memory Model
* Capability Model
* Kernel Blueprint

---

## Inputs

* Seed Package

---

## Outputs

* Seed Validation Report

---

## Failure Consequence

Germination is aborted.

The individual is never created.

---

# Stage 2 — Kernel Birth

## Purpose

Instantiate the Kernel.

This is the moment of biological birth.

The individual now exists.

---

## Actions

Instantiate:

* Identity Core
* Self Model
* Mission Engine
* Survival Core
* Policy Core
* Stage Controller
* Boundary Engine
* Trust Engine
* Capability Loader
* Event Loop

---

## Outputs

* Kernel Alive

---

## Validation

The individual must answer:

* Who am I?
* What species do I belong to?
* What is my mission?

---

## Failure Consequence

Birth failure.

The individual is considered non-viable.

---

## Purpose

* Nest Identifier

---

## Failure Consequence

---

# Stage 4 — Observation Phase

## Purpose

Observe reality.

No actions are allowed.

No modifications are allowed.

No assumptions are allowed.

---

## Active Systems

* Root System
* Discovery Intelligence
* Classification Intelligence

---

## Allowed Activities

* Read
* Observe
* Collect
* Record

---

## Forbidden Activities

* Execute
* Modify
* Repair
* Adapt

---

## Outputs

* Evidence Collection
* Candidate Objects
* Candidate Capabilities

---

## Failure Consequence

The individual develops unreliable knowledge.

---

No predefined habitat assumptions are allowed.

* Habitat Graph

---

* Capability Coverage

## Outputs

# Germination Success Criteria

A germination is considered successful only if:

* Kernel is alive
* Identity exists
* Mission is loaded
* Observation has occurred
* DNA exists
* Habitat Graph exists
* germ_* structures exist
* NOTOUCH stage is reached

---

# Germination Failure Criteria

Germination fails if:

* Kernel cannot start
* Identity cannot be established
* DNA cannot be generated
* Evidence cannot be collected
* Adaptations cannot be generated

# Species Continuity Rule

Every individual must be capable of repeating the Germination Model.

A capability belongs to the species only if it can survive:

Individual

↓

Seed Export

↓

Nest Destruction

↓

Seed Planting

↓

Germination

↓

New Individual

↓

Capability Reappearance

If a capability cannot survive this cycle, it does not belong to the species.

No individual may inherit understanding.

Understanding must always be earned through observation.

The Seed inherits knowledge.

The individual earns wisdom.
