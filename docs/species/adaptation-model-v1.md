# PHYTCOI Adaptation Model v1

## Purpose

The Adaptation Model defines how a PHYTCOI individual develops habitat-specific structures.

The purpose of Adaptation is to transform understanding into operational capability.

Observation creates evidence.

Validation creates DNA.

Understanding creates a World Model.

Adaptation creates usable structures.

Without Adaptation, an individual may understand reality but cannot effectively interact with it.

---

# Fundamental Principle

The Genome is universal.

The Habitat is local.

Adaptation is the bridge between them.

Adaptations allow a universal species to operate inside a specific habitat without embedding habitat-specific knowledge inside the Genome.

---

# Core Question

The Adaptation Model answers:

How do I transform habitat understanding into operational capability?

---

# Definition

An Adaptation is a habitat-specific structure generated from validated knowledge.

Adaptations may include:

* drivers
* connectors
* translators
* capability bindings
* governance bindings
* workflow bindings
* operational policies

Adaptations are generated.

They are not inherited.

# Adaptation Sources

Adaptations may be generated from:

* validated DNA entries
* World Model understanding
* Experience records
* recurring operational needs
* governance requirements

All adaptation sources must be traceable.

# Adaptation Objectives

Adaptations exist to:

* improve habitat interaction
* improve operational efficiency
* improve governance quality
* improve observability
* improve survivability

Adaptations must never exist for their own sake.

# Adaptation Classes

The species recognizes multiple adaptation classes.

---

## Driver Adaptations

Purpose:

Enable interaction with habitat objects.

Examples:

* germ_hypervisor_driver
* germ_identity_driver
* germ_storage_driver
* germ_dns_driver

---

## Capability Adaptations

Purpose:

Expose operational capabilities.

Examples:

* backup capability
* restart capability
* migration capability

---

## Governance Adaptations

Purpose:

Apply governance rules.

Examples:

* approval workflows
* trust workflows
* risk workflows

---

## Observation Adaptations

Purpose:

Improve evidence collection.

Examples:

* sensors
* discovery extensions
* telemetry integrations

---

## Recovery Adaptations

Purpose:

Improve resilience.

Examples:

* rollback automation
* continuity procedures
* recovery workflows

---

# Adaptation Naming

All habitat-specific adaptations must use:

```text
germ_*
```

prefixes.

Examples:

germ_dns_driver

germ_identity_driver

germ_storage_provider_adapter

The prefix indicates habitat-derived structures.

---

# Adaptation Requirements

Every adaptation must declare:

* purpose
* source knowledge
* expected benefit
* associated risks
* confidence level
* lifecycle state

No anonymous adaptations are permitted.

---

# Adaptation Validation

Before activation, an adaptation must be validated.

Validation includes:

* structural validation
* safety validation
* policy validation
* trust validation
* rollback validation

Unsafe adaptations must remain inactive.

---

# Adaptation Confidence

Adaptations possess confidence levels.

Confidence increases through:

* successful operation
* repeated validation
* positive experience

Confidence decreases through:

* failures
* contradictions
* unexpected outcomes

Confidence influences trust.

---

# Adaptation Evolution

Adaptations are living structures.

An adaptation may:

* improve
* expand
* contract
* specialize
* merge
* retire

Adaptation evolution must remain traceable.

---

# Adaptation and DNA

DNA provides truth.

Adaptations consume truth.

Adaptations do not modify DNA.

Adaptations depend on DNA.

DNA remains authoritative.

---

# Adaptation and World Model

The World Model provides understanding.

Adaptations use understanding to determine:

* where interaction is needed
* how interaction should occur
* what consequences may result

Understanding guides adaptation.

---

# Adaptation and Experience

Experience provides operational lessons.

Experience may:

* strengthen adaptations
* weaken adaptations
* trigger adaptation redesign
* trigger retirement

Experience drives maturity.

---

# Adaptation and Governance

Every adaptation must respect:

* Constitution
* Mission
* Lifecycle
* Trust Boundaries
* Risk Boundaries

No adaptation may bypass governance.

# Adaptation Failure

Failure conditions include:

* unsupported adaptation
* unsafe adaptation

* orphan adaptation

Adaptation failure increases operational risk.

# Adaptation Recovery

Recovery mechanisms include:

* rollback
* redesign
* revalidation

* retirement

The species maintains an Adaptation Registry.

The registry contains:

* active adaptations
* proposed adaptations
* retired adaptations
* validation history
* confidence history

The registry enables adaptation governance.

---

# Adaptation Principle

Adaptation is not customization.

Adaptation is biological growth.

A customization changes behavior manually.

An adaptation emerges from understanding.

PHYTCOI grows adaptations because it understands its habitat.

---

# Final Rule

A PHYTCOI individual must remain universal at birth.

Adaptations allow that individual to become effective within a specific habitat.

The Genome defines what the species can become.

Adaptations define how the individual survives where it lives.

Without Adaptation there is no growth.

Without growth there is no maturity.
