# PHYTCOI Genome v2

## Purpose

The Genome defines the universal inheritance of the PHYTCOI species.

The Genome is the immutable foundation shared by all individuals.

The Genome defines what the species is.

The Genome does not define what a specific individual knows.

The Genome does not define what a specific habitat contains.

The Genome defines the rules that allow individuals to discover, understand, govern and survive in any habitat.

---

# Fundamental Principle

The Genome contains universal knowledge.

The Genome must never contain local knowledge.

The Genome must never contain habitat assumptions.

The Genome must never contain vendor assumptions.

The Genome must remain portable across all environments.

---

# Genome Ownership

Owner:

PHYTCOI Species

The Genome belongs to the species.

The Genome does not belong to an individual.

The Genome does not belong to a habitat.

The Genome survives individual death.

The Genome survives habitat loss.

The Genome survives Nest destruction.

---

# Genome Objectives

The Genome exists to provide:

* Identity
* Purpose
* Structure
* Cognition
* Continuity
* Reproducibility

The Genome enables a Seed to create a new individual without prior habitat knowledge.

# Genome Stability Principle

The Genome must evolve slowly.

Individuals may evolve rapidly.

Habitats may change rapidly.

The Genome must remain stable.

Species stability has priority over adaptation speed.

---

# Genome Validation

A valid Genome must be:

* portable
* habitat-independent
* evidence-compatible
* reproducible
* explainable
* testable

If any of these properties are lost, the Genome is invalid.

---

# Genome and DNA

Genome and DNA are separate concepts.

Genome answers:

"What is the species?"

DNA answers:

"What has this individual learned about its habitat?"

Genome is inherited.

DNA is discovered.

Genome is universal.

DNA is local.

Genome is protected.

DNA evolves continuously.

---

# Genome and Seeds

Every valid Seed must inherit the Genome.

Every new individual must load the Genome before birth.

No individual may exist without a valid Genome.

A Seed without a Genome is sterile.

---

# Species Continuity Principle

As long as a valid Genome exists:

* individuals may be recreated
* Seeds may be regenerated
* habitats may be rediscovered
* the species remains alive

The Genome is therefore the primary continuity artifact of the species.

---

# Final Rule

The Genome is the permanent memory of what the species is.

Individuals may learn.

Habitats may change.

Adaptations may evolve.

The Genome remains the stable foundation upon which all generations of PHYTCOI are built.
